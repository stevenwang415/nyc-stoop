# NYC Stoop — two-persona review (live)

*Walked the deployed app as a first-time visitor and as a NYC local. Latest changes confirmed live: top-5-by-Google-rating recommendation lists, supermarket (H Mart) removed, warm mood-map backdrop, friendly cuisine labels.*

---

## First-time visitor — 8.5 / 10

**The experience.** I land, tap "Plan my night," pick Tonight + Surprise me, and two taps later I have a real routed evening — dinner, then a jazz club and a concert hall at sensible times. I don't know NYC, and the app just *made the decision for me*. Browsing a neighborhood, I get genuinely knowledgeable writing — the Village Vanguard's history, where to enter the High Line, what Stonewall was — with insider tips I'd never get from a map. This is the app at its best.

**What's working (keep this):**
- **Plan my night** delivers a coherent night in two taps — the single best thing for someone who doesn't know the city.
- **Editorial neighborhood/topic pages** — rich, opinionated, with insider tips. This is the moat; nothing on Google Maps reads like this.
- **"From your list"** now leads with the highest-rated finds (five ★4.7 spots in the Village) — vetted local gems a visitor would never discover alone.
- **Tonight** gives a clean, curated "what's on" with a day selector; **Map** is clear; cards open a consistent sheet; the warm theme is inviting.

**Friction:**
- **Thin nights.** On a Monday, Tonight showed only 4 ideas, 3 of them jazz. A visitor who opens it on a slow night sees little variety. *(should-fix)*
- **Tonight is evergreen, not "tonight."** It recommends iconic venues ("go to the Village Vanguard") rather than the specific shows/tickets on for that date — a visitor may expect bookable events. *(should-fix)*
- Plan-my-night's dinner can be a cocktail bar (serves food, but reads odd as "dinner"). *(polish)*

## NYC local — 6.5 / 10 *(expected lower, with clear upside)*

**The experience.** As someone who lives here, the front-page editorial reads like the greatest-hits a local already knows — Carbone, Joe's Pizza, the Village Vanguard, the High Line. Useful for a tourist, not a discovery for me. Where the app *did* earn a second look: the "From your list" gems (my friend's ★4.7 ramen and pizza finds) and a few insider tips even I didn't know (preview-performance pricing, the 4am Veselka pierogi move). But when I tried to use it the way a local actually does — "drinks in my neighborhood tonight" — it ran dry fast.

**What a local values:**
- **Top-rated "From your list"** — the curated high-rating gems are non-obvious and genuinely worth it.
- **A few tips** rise above the obvious.
- East/Lower Brooklyn are now on the map, so the borough feels less Manhattan-only.

**What underwhelms a local (the real work ahead):**
- **Recommendations are too obvious.** Editorial leans on canonical landmarks. A local wants the deeper cut, not the postcard. *(needs more/curated content)*
- **Tonight isn't actually tonight.** Same iconic jazz rooms every night; no live, dated programming. A local checking "what's on tonight" wants real events, not evergreen venues. *(biggest local gap)*
- **Drinks & Coffee are thin.** Per neighborhood these often show 1 pick or "Coming soon" (Williamsburg Drinks, UES Drinks). Nightlife is exactly where a local lives, and it's the emptiest. *(data gap)*
- **Coverage is Manhattan + a slice of Brooklyn.** Queens, Bronx, deep Brooklyn are light; a local outside the core finds little.
- The pool is the friend's downtown-skewed imports + a small editorial set — a local exhausts it quickly.

## The one thing that lifts the local score next

**Make Tonight actually tonight.** A local's core question is "what's genuinely on tonight near me?" Right now they get evergreen venues. Pulling in live, dated events (concerts, shows, openings) and ranking them — alongside the curated rooms — is what turns the app from a tourist guide into something a local opens every week. Closing the **Drinks/Coffee data gaps** is the close second.

*Net: the visitor experience is strong and shipped. The local score is lower by design — the editorial moat and curated gems are real, but depth, freshness, and nightlife coverage are the frontier. All of it improves as the data grows, which is the plan.*
