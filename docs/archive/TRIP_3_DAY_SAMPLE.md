# 3-Day NYC Sampler — Hits, Hidden, Heart

A walkable itinerary for a first-time visitor who wants the postcard NYC *and* the moments most tourists miss. Built from venues currently in the NYC Stoop database. Designed for two travelers in shoulder season; swap food for budget as needed.

---

## Day 1 — Midtown Classic + a Broadway Night

**Theme:** the canonical Manhattan day. Iconic landmarks, a great Italian dinner, blockbuster show, jazz nightcap.

### Morning · 9:00–12:00
- **9:00am — Grand Central Terminal** (Midtown East). Walk the main concourse, look up at the celestial ceiling, then sneak into the **Whispering Gallery** (downstairs by the Oyster Bar — stand at one corner, your partner at the diagonal opposite, whisper). 30 min.
- **9:45am — NYPL Schwarzman Building** (5 min walk). The Rose Main Reading Room is one of the most beautiful indoor spaces in America. Free. 30 min.
- **10:30am — Bryant Park** (across the street). Coffee from any kiosk, watch the city wake up. 20 min.
- **11:00am — MoMA** (11 min walk up 6th Ave). Floor 5: the Picasso / Matisse / Van Gogh / Pollock circuit — *Starry Night*, *Demoiselles d'Avignon*, *Number 31*. Skip floors 1–3 if pressed. Buy a timed ticket online to skip the line. **$30, ~1.5 hrs.**

### Lunch · 12:30
- **The Modern (inside MoMA)** for sit-down, or — better — walk 10 min to **Chelsea Market** (75 9th Ave). Eat at Los Tacos No. 1 or Hale & Hearty. Use the bathroom; you won't see another good one for a while.

### Afternoon · 2:00–6:00
- **2:00pm — High Line entrance at Gansevoort St**. Walk north all the way to 34th St (1.45 mi, ~50 min). Stop at the **30th St amphitheater seats** to watch traffic on 10th Ave below. The chaise loungers near the 14th St elevator are the best free seats in the city if it's sunny.
- **3:30pm — Hudson Yards / The Vessel area for a coffee break.**
- **4:00pm — Empire State Building** (subway 7 to 34th St-Hudson Yards → walk; or just walk 15 min). 86th floor observatory at sunset hour. ⚠️ Buy timed entry online, lines are real. **$44, 1.5 hrs.**

### Dinner · 7:00pm
- **Carbone** (181 Thompson St, West Village). Spicy rigatoni vodka, veal parmesan. Booking via Resy was virtually impossible — try the bar walk-in around 5:30pm if no reso.
- **Backup:** **Don Angie** (103 Greenwich Ave) — stuffed Sunday gravy lasagna. Easier to book.

### Evening · 8:00pm
- **Curtain at 8:00pm — Hamilton at the Richard Rodgers Theatre** (226 W 46th St). Enter the daily lottery via the official Hamilton app — $10 seats, drawn 2 hours before curtain.

### Late · 11:00pm
- **Village Vanguard** (178 7th Ave South). The 11pm set is shorter and feels more local than the 8pm. $40 cover, two-drink minimum. *Cash only*. Closest subway: 14 St / 7 Ave.

**Total walk: ~5 miles. Budget: $250–400/pp incl. show.**

## Day 2 — Brooklyn Day, Brunch to Late Night

**Theme:** outer-borough realness. Park, museum, butchery, a Brooklyn-style night.

### Brunch · 10:00am
- **Russ & Daughters Café** (127 Orchard St, Lower East Side — yes, technically Manhattan but it's the right gateway). Smoked sable on a bagel, latkes, egg cream. Easier to book than the original counter location.
- Subway: F to 2 Av, walk 4 blocks. 1.5 hrs.

### Midday · 12:00–4:00
- **12:30pm — Brooklyn Bridge walk** (start at the Manhattan side, walk to Brooklyn). 1 mile, 30 min. The center walkway is for pedestrians; stick to the white-painted line and the bikers won't yell.
- **1:15pm — DUMBO**. Take the photo at the corner of Washington & Water Streets (the Manhattan Bridge framed between brick warehouses).
- **1:30pm — Jane's Carousel** in **Brooklyn Bridge Park** — 1920s wooden carousel inside a glass cube on the water. $2 ride or just walk through.
- **2:00pm — Brooklyn Museum** (200 Eastern Pkwy). Egyptian wing is world-class; the rooftop Feminist Art Center is unique. Combine with **Brooklyn Botanic Garden** next door if you have stamina. $20. 2 hrs.

### Late Afternoon · 4:30
- **Prospect Park** for a walk. Enter at Grand Army Plaza; head for the **Long Meadow** — Manhattan has Central Park, but locals come here.

### Dinner · 7:30pm
- **Peter Luger** (178 Broadway, Williamsburg) — porterhouse for two, creamed spinach, German fried potatoes. Cash or their own card only. *Book a month ahead*.
- **Backup:** **Lilia** (567 Union Ave) — Missy Robbins' agnolotti. Resy opens 30 days out at 10am sharp; set a calendar reminder.

### Evening · 9:30
- Walk 10 min to **Domino Park** — view of the Williamsburg Bridge lit at night, taco/oyster vendors. Free.
- Or grab a cocktail at **Death & Co** (433 E 6th St) back in the East Village — a 25-min Uber.

**Total walk: ~4 miles. Budget: $200–350/pp.**

## Day 3 — Uptown Culture, Park, and a Quieter Night

**Theme:** the side of NYC the locals defend. Museum Mile, Central Park interior, classical music.

### Morning · 9:30
- **Bagel breakfast at H&H Bagels** or any local — fuel up.
- **10:00am — Metropolitan Museum of Art** (1000 Fifth Ave). Egyptian wing → Temple of Dendur, American Wing → Tiffany Mosaic, European Paintings → Vermeer's *Young Woman with a Water Pitcher*. 3 hours. **$30 pay-what-you-wish for NY state residents; $30 fixed otherwise.**

### Lunch · 1:30
- **Levain Bakery** (167 W 74th — about 12 min taxi south, 25 min walk through Central Park). The chocolate chip walnut cookie is a religion. Eat on a Sheep Meadow blanket.

### Afternoon · 2:30–5:00
- **Sheep Meadow** in Central Park. Read, nap, watch the city not move.
- **3:30pm — Bow Bridge** photo walk. Then north along **the Lake**.
- **4:00pm — Central Park → AMNH (American Museum of Natural History)**. Hayden Planetarium is the can't-miss. Dinosaurs are the second can't-miss. **$28, 1.5 hrs.**

### Dinner · 6:30pm
- **Lincoln Center area** — eat at **The Smith** or **Bar Boulud** for something pre-theater-paced.

### Evening · 7:30pm
- **Carnegie Hall** spring season (881 7th Ave). Whatever's on. Even if you don't know classical music, do this once — the room is the instrument. **$30 rear balcony seats are honestly some of the best in the house. ~2 hrs.**

### Late · 10:30pm
- **The Campbell** inside Grand Central Terminal (15 Vanderbilt Ave). 1923 banker's office turned cocktail bar, all carved walnut and leaded glass. Skip the line by entering through the Vanderbilt Ave doors, not 42nd St.

**Total walk: ~4 miles. Budget: $200–400/pp incl. concert.**

## Logistics

**Subway:** Get an OMNY-tap ride or a 7-day unlimited MetroCard ($34). Hit the 14-day cap at $34 weekly fare — uses any subway/bus.

**Don't:**
- Don't try to do dinner *before* a Broadway show without a reservation that ends by 7pm. The wait crushes you.
- Don't drive. Anywhere. Ever.
- Don't book MoMA, ESB, or Met tickets at the door — buy timed entry online, save 30 minutes of standing.

**Three things this itinerary skips on purpose:**
- Statue of Liberty / Ellis Island (a half-day commitment; great but high-friction)
- Times Square (you'll walk through it twice anyway — for theater on Day 1 and to/from the subway often)
- 9/11 Memorial & One WTC Observatory (deserves its own slow morning — add as a half-day if extending)

**If you only had two days:** combine Day 1 + Day 3 into one (skip Empire State, skip Carnegie), and do Day 2 as Day 2.

**If you had four days:** add a Lower Manhattan day — 9/11 Memorial, Statue of Liberty ferry, Tenement Museum, Fraunces Tavern, dinner in Tribeca, Smalls Jazz Club for a 1am set.
