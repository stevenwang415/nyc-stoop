Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 29, 2026

NYC Stoop ("we", "the app") is built with privacy as the default. This page explains what data the app handles, what it doesn't, and how you can contact us with questions.

Short version: NYC Stoop runs entirely on your device. We don't collect, store, or transmit any personal information. Your saves, trip plans, notes, and custom places live in your device's local storage and never leave it.

What we don't collect

  • No account is required to use NYC Stoop.
  • We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information.
  • We do not track your location.
  • We do not use third-party analytics or advertising SDKs.
  • We do not sell or share any data with third parties (because we don't have any to sell or share).

What stays on your device

The following information is stored in your device's local storage (the iOS equivalent of browser localStorage) so the app can remember your preferences between launches:

  • Venues, works, and sights you've saved with the heart button.
  • Custom places you add via "+ Add" (name, blurb, category, neighborhood, optional address and hours).
  • Your trip plan: which venues are scheduled, day overrides, cuisine choices per meal, notes attached to stops.
  • Your onboarding status (so we don't show it again).

None of this leaves your device. If you uninstall NYC Stoop, this data is deleted with the app. If you want to clear it manually while keeping the app installed, use the "Clear all data" option in Settings.

Third-party services

The map view loads tiles from OpenStreetMap, a public collaborative mapping project. When you view the map, your device requests tiles from OpenStreetMap's servers. OpenStreetMap logs these requests for operational purposes; see their privacy policy for details. We do not send any user identifiers or saved data along with these requests.

Some external links in the app (ticket booking, restaurant reservations, official venue websites) open in your device's default browser. Once you leave the app, those websites' own privacy policies apply.

Children's privacy

NYC Stoop is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone of any age — including children.

Changes to this policy

If our data practices change in a future version (for example, if we add optional accounts and cloud sync), we'll update this page and bump the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be flagged in the app's "What's new" notes for the affected release.
