# NYC Stoop — UX review

*Reviewed live at nyc-stoop.vercel.app, mobile frame, Thursday Jun 18 2026. Walked end-to-end as three personas, exercising every surface by hand.*

---

## How each persona experienced it

**First-time tourist, one evening.** I tapped the big "Plan my night" button, chose *Tonight* + *Surprise me*, and hit Build my plan. I landed on a two-day itinerary of **morning** museums — Empire State 10:45am, Times Square, MoMA — with nothing about tonight and no evening anywhere in it. The plan itself, once I read it, was lovely (timed stops, walk times, a real lunch pick). But I asked for a night and got daytime sightseeing across Thursday *and* Friday. The promise and the result didn't match, and I was dropped straight into a dense trip-management screen rather than a clean "here's your night."

**Returning local checking Tonight.** This is the strongest tab. A day selector across the week with a live count per night, clean category filters, and sections for Evening / Late Night / Daytime / In Season. Switching to Friday genuinely changed the lineup (it added the Met's Egyptian Wing, Fri–Sat only). Tapping a card opened a beautiful detail page with a schedule, and the back arrow correctly returned me to Tonight. It felt curated and trustworthy — until I noticed cards still say "Tonight" while I'm browsing Friday.

**Planning a weekend with friends.** The editorial is the reason to use this over Google Maps. Browsing Greenwich Village gave me categorized picks with real writing and insider tips, plus a clearly-separated "From your list" layer of my own saved places. Search even surfaced the albums recorded at a venue. The let-down was the home "This Week in NYC" feed, which mixed real events with raw city-permit noise, and the My Trip screen felt sluggish.

## What's working — keep this

- **The editorial voice is the moat.** The jazz-clubs essay, the per-venue blurbs, and the 💡 insider tips ("Bar seats are first-come walk-in — show up 5pm") are genuinely good travel writing. Nothing Google Maps does comes close. Protect this.
- **Venue detail pages are excellent** — facts strip, "Why NYC Stoop picked this," a schedule-aware Upcoming list, official-site ticket links (not Ticketmaster), "+ Add to My Trip," Also Nearby, and real photos that load.
- **Tonight** — day selector with per-night counts, accurate filter counts, real day-to-day variation, Hamilton correctly showing "Tue–Sun (dark Mondays)," and back-nav that returns you to the feed.
- **Search** spans venues *and* related works (Sonny Rollins / Bill Evans Vanguard albums) — a thoughtful, distinctive touch.
- **Neighborhood browse** — categorized editorial picks with a cleanly labeled "From your list" personal layer ("not NYC Stoop editorial picks").
- **The itinerary UI itself** — timed stops, walk-time between each, prices, and specific restaurant picks (The Modern, Caravaggio) with Reserve + Maps links. The Map view's color-coded pins + legend + saved markers are solid.

## Issues & friction

### Must-fix

1. **"Plan my night" ignores its own inputs and contradicts its name.** I chose *Tonight* + *Surprise me* and received a two-day, all-morning museum plan built from saved places. The WHEN/WHERE chooser had no visible effect, and the result has no evening content at all. This is the marquee CTA, and the promise→delivery gap is the single biggest trust breaker for a first-timer. *Fix: when "Tonight" is selected, build an evening plan (use the Tonight feed's evening/late-night inventory); honor the neighborhood choice; and end on a clean plan summary, not the full management screen.*

2. **Checklist and Full plan disagree on times for the same stops.** Checklist showed Times Square 10:00am / MoMA 12:45pm; Full plan showed Times Square 12:25pm / MoMA 2:05pm. The Checklist order is also wrong (Empire 10:45 listed before Times Square 10:00) despite the on-screen claim "ordered through the day." Two views of the same plan that don't match read as broken. *Fix: compute the schedule once and render both views from it.*

3. **The "This Week in NYC" live feed surfaces non-events.** Alongside real items it lists "Cherry Lawn Closure" (a park *closure*), "RIPA Special Event Permit," "PARKS EVENT," and "FWC2026" whose location is a raw block-by-block street-segment dump. Presenting permit/closure records as things to do erodes trust and looks broken next to the polished editorial. *Fix: filter the Open Data feed to genuine public events and clean titles/locations before display.*

### Should-fix

4. **Performance on My Trip.** With 494 imported places loaded, the screen is sluggish — the renderer repeatedly stalled during the review. This is the core planning surface, so the lag is costly. *Fix: virtualize/paginate "Your places" and defer rendering the 494-item list.*

5. **Meal cuisine filter didn't visibly work.** Selecting "Korean" left the suggestion as The Modern; I couldn't confirm the pick re-filters by cuisine. *Worth verifying — if there's no match it should say so rather than silently keep an unrelated spot.*

6. **Category/label data bugs.** "Joe's Pizza" is tagged **Visual Art** in Also-Nearby. On the Map, the filter chip says "Music" while the legend says "Classical." Small, but they undercut the otherwise-careful curation.

7. **Relative-date copy is static.** Cards say "Tonight" / "Today" even when viewing Friday's lineup. Should read "Fri" (or "that night") for non-today selections.

8. **Interaction consistency on Tonight.** Tapping a curated card opens a full page. The intended pattern was a bottom sheet for all Tonight cards; no live "On Sale" events were present to compare today, but the curated picks currently go full-page.

### Polish

9. **Nav active-state.** On first load, no tab reads as active and "Tonight" appears permanently accented, so the bottom nav doesn't reliably show which screen you're on.
10. **Truncation cuts mid-word** — several insider tips and blurbs end abruptly ("Los Tacos No."). Trim on a word boundary with an ellipsis.
11. **Loose neighborhood grouping** — Chelsea Market and NoHo venues filed under "Greenwich Village"; Don Angie appears in both the editorial list and "From your list."
12. **Seasonal staleness** — "Carnegie Hall spring season" shown in mid-June.
13. **Map** — neighborhood labels are tiny abbreviations (FIDI, LES, C.P., UES) a tourist won't decode; the schematic is Manhattan-only; tapping a neighborhood jumps to the full map rather than zooming to it.
14. **"Add to My Trip"** exists but sits mid-page inside "Plan your visit." Given it's a primary action, consider a sticky CTA.
15. **A tab named "Tonight" contains "Daytime" and "In Season" sections** — useful content, slightly at odds with the name.

| Area | Score |

| Editorial content & voice | 9 / 10 |
| Venue detail pages | 9 / 10 |
| Discovery (Explore / Search / Browse) | 8 / 10 |
| Tonight feed | 7.5 / 10 |
| Map | 7 / 10 |

| Plan my night → itinerary | 5 / 10 |
| "This Week" live data quality | 4 / 10 |
| Performance & reliability | 5 / 10 |
| **Overall** | **7 / 10** |

A 7 that's really a 9 wearing a 5: the content and detail pages are genuinely special, but the headline "Plan my night" flow and the auto-fed data drag the whole experience down because they're the first things a new user touches.

## The one thing I'd fix first

**Make "Plan my night" deliver on its name.** When a user picks *Tonight*, build an evening plan from the Tonight inventory in the chosen neighborhood, reconcile the Checklist/Full-plan times, and finish on a clean "here's your night" summary instead of dumping them into the 494-place management screen. It's the most-tapped entry point, the promise is currently broken, and fixing it converts the app's best asset — the curation — into the payoff a first-time user actually feels.
